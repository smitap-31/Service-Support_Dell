KYHD Keep Your Hard Drive

Retain physical control over sensitive, proprietary and confidential data while maintaining your limited warranty1 protection.

No one likes the thought of a failed hard drive, especially in a world where sensitive data on that hard drive could

be at increased risk of exploitation. New and sophisticated data recovery technologies have made enormous amounts of confidential information vulnerable, even on malfunctioning drives. In addition, national security issues and data privacy regulations dictate greater-than-ever attention to security measures. As a result, many companies now choose to retain possession of their failed hard drives instead of returning them to the manufacturer during replace- ment, a procedure that can conflict with many standard warranty agreements. Maintaining data security has become a paramount concern.

Dell has a solution. The Keep Your Hard Drive (KYHD) service puts Dell customers from small businesses to large public companies and government agencies in complete control of their data, allowing them to keep failed hard drives (still covered by limited warranty1) when replacement hard drives are delivered. Its a unique enhancement to the Dell Limited Warranty1 on Failed Hard Drives that puts you in charge of your data and hard drive disposal.

Its simply smart business to closely manage access to your data. The Dell KYHD service, available for desktops and notebooks, workstations, servers and storage systems, allows you to do it with confidence.

The KYHD SERVICE

More Security for Dell Customers

Designed to enable organizations to retain physical control over sensitive data, while preserving all the benefits of the Dell Limited Warranty1 on Failed Hard Drives. Customers are required to return the drive (still covered by limited warranty1) to Dell when receiving replacements at no additional charge. The KYHD service waives that requirement, allowing for maximum data control, security and disposal options.

How to Get It

The Keep Your Hard Drive service is available at the time of system purchase or anytime before your system experiences a hard drive failure, while the system remains under limited warranty1. Currently not available for Dell Home Sales. Please contact your Dell sales representative for more details.
